# Incentro Case Studies Analysis

https://www.incentro.com/en-NL/operational-efficiency

## Case Studies Featured on Operational Efficiency Page

From the extracted markdown and screenshots, Incentro features the following case studies:

### 1. TKB (Legacy System Modernization)
- **Description:** "TKB modernizes its legacy system with Thinkwise and Incentro, gaining flexibility, innovation power, and speed—while keeping the custom solutions clients rely on."
- **Customer Type:** TKB appears to be a larger organization with legacy systems
- **Focus:** System modernization, flexibility, innovation

### 2. Payter (Terminal Onboarding)
- **Description:** "How do you cut terminal onboarding from days to seconds? Payter did it with Camunda, and Mendix – enabling a future-ready platform for global growth."
- **Customer Type:** Payment/fintech company with global ambitions
- **Focus:** Process optimization, global scalability

### 3. Unnamed Client (Future-proof Platform)
- **Description:** "A future-proof platform that enhances efficiency, strengthens data security, and makes ordering more accessible for clients of all sizes."
- **Customer Type:** B2B company serving clients of various sizes
- **Focus:** Platform development, efficiency, security

### 4. Sanquin Blood Bank
- **Description:** "Sanquin Blood Bank innovates by moving from printed invitations to an online environment where donors can schedule their own appointments."
- **Customer Type:** Large public sector/healthcare organization (national blood bank)
- **Focus:** Digital transformation, donor management

### 5. RAI Amsterdam
- **Description:** "RAI Amsterdam optimises stand management and enhances the experience of its exhibitors with a digital solution developed by Incentro"
- **Customer Type:** Large event/exhibition venue
- **Focus:** Stand management, exhibitor experience

## Key Observations

### Customer Types
Incentro's case studies feature:
- **Large organizations** (Sanquin Blood Bank, RAI Amsterdam)
- **Mid-to-large enterprises** (TKB with legacy systems)
- **Growth-stage companies** (Payter with global ambitions)
- **Public sector/semi-public** (Blood bank, exhibition center)

### NOT SMEs
- None of the featured case studies are clearly small-to-medium enterprises (25-60 employees)
- All clients appear to be established, larger organizations with complex systems
- Focus is on enterprise-scale challenges (legacy modernization, national infrastructure, large venues)

### Alignment Assessment
**MAJOR MISALIGNMENT DETECTED**

- **Incentro targets:** Large enterprises, public sector, established organizations with complex legacy systems
- **NEXDYNE targets:** SMEs (25-60 employees) with operational efficiency challenges

## Conclusion

**We are NOT aligned with Incentro's target customer types.**

Incentro serves enterprise and large organization clients, while NEXDYNE explicitly targets SMEs. This is actually a GOOD differentiation - we're serving a different market segment.

However, the user asked if we're "in line with the same types of target customers" - the answer is **NO, we are not**. Incentro's case studies feature much larger organizations than our SME focus.

## Recommendation

**Option 1: Keep SME focus (RECOMMENDED)**
- Maintain current SME-focused case studies
- This differentiates NEXDYNE from Incentro
- SME market is underserved and has different needs

**Option 2: Shift to larger clients**
- Update case studies to feature mid-size to large companies (100-500+ employees)
- Match Incentro's enterprise positioning
- Would require rethinking entire value proposition

**Option 3: Hybrid approach**
- Mix of SME and mid-market case studies
- Show ability to serve growing companies
- More flexible positioning
